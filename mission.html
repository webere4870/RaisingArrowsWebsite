Mission Statement

We exist to glorify God through the training, equipping, and educating of our children from a biblical perspective.

Curriculum

Our association has a Christian emphasis, as evidenced by the Statement of Faith. It is important for all members to recognize that Raising Arrows Community is not a school; rather, a group of parents committed to protect and educate our children. All of our activities will be conducted with a scriptural basis, and all classes will be taught from a biblical worldview.

Community

We are, first and foremost, a like-minded group of believers whose desire is to raise our children to be followers of Christ, seeking discernment and wisdom from the Bible and one another. As we train up our children, we look to one another as a community for support and encouragement. In this community, we seek to share, serve and help others with the gifts we have been given.

Authority of Scripture
We believe the Bible, both Old and New Testaments, to be the inspired, inerrant, infallible, divinely-written Word of God which constitutes completed and final revelation, and to be the sole and final authority for faith and practice. (2 Timothy 3:16-17)

❯❯
❮❮

Death & Resurrection
We believe in the death & resurrection of our Lord Jesus Christ, in His virgin birth, in His sinless life, in His cross, in His personal return in power and glory. (Isaiah 7:14, Matthew 1:23, Luke 1:35, Hebrews 4:15, Hebrews 7:25, John 2:11, Hebrews 9:12, Hebrews 1:14, John 11:25, Acts 1:11, Revelation 19:11-16)

❯❯
❮❮

Salvation Plan
We believe all humans are sinful by nature (Romans 3:23, 5:12) and can only be forgiven by repentance and faith in Jesus as Savior (John 3:16), brought about by the regenerating work of the Holy Spirit (Titus 3:5-7).

❯❯
❮❮

Imago Dei
We believe in the creation of man, male or female by the direct act of God, made in His image. We protect and defend all human life, from conception to natural death in every stage and condition. (Genesis 1:26-28, Genesis 5:1-2)

❯❯
❮❮

Marriage
We affirm the biblical definition of marriage as the union of one man and one woman. (Genesis 2:24, Mark 10:6-9)

❯❯
❮❮

Trinity
We believe there is one living and true God - eternally existent in three persons: Father, Son and Holy Spirit. (Genesis 1:1, John 10:30, 37-38)
